# Hypoteka.info - Business Assessment & Plan

## Context

Financial literacy in Czechia is low (~48% financially literate), mortgage/housing knowledge is fragmented across bank calculators and broker sites, and Czechia is **Europe's least affordable housing market** (13.3x annual salary for an apartment). Existing tools are shallow "monthly payment" calculators. There's no Czech equivalent of CFPB or comprehensive mortgage education platform.

The goal: a non-profit, LLM-curated (human-reviewed) informational website helping Czechs understand mortgages, make better decisions, and overcome misconceptions.

---

## 0. Proof It Works: Successful Projects in Other Countries

| Project | Country | Scale | How They Grew (no ads) | Revenue Model |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **MoneySavingExpert** | UK | 16M monthly users, 14M email subs, 1.3M forum members | Started with £100 in 2003. Pure SEO + word-of-mouth + community forums. "Consumer revenge" tone went viral. | Affiliate commissions (no paid ads on site) |
| **Finanztip.de** | Germany | 3.1M monthly visits, 750K newsletter subs, 100 employees | 70% organic search traffic. 1,000+ detailed guides. Non-profit structure builds trust. | $20.5M/yr revenue (affiliate + donations) |
| **Unbiased.co.uk** | UK | 10M+ users/year | Referral marketplace connecting users to 27K financial advisers. | Adviser subscription fees |
| **CFPB Tools** | US | 63M+ users of "Ask CFPB", 45K calls/month | Government authority + "Know Before You Owe" campaign. Calculators embedded everywhere. | Government funded |
| **Khan Academy (finance)** | Global | 120M yearly learners total, 31.5M visits/month | YouTube discovery -> teacher recommendations -> organic growth. | Non-profit donations |

**Key insight:** None of these used paid ads as primary growth channel. They all grew through **trust + genuinely useful tools + community**. MoneySavingExpert is the most relevant model - one person, started with nothing, built through authentic voice and useful content.

**Czech/Slovak gap:** No equivalent exists in CZ or SK. Poland has some basic tools but nothing comprehensive. This is genuinely unoccupied territory.

## 1. Does This Make Sense? YES - With Caveats

### Why YES:
- **Real gap exists.** No Czech site combines: advanced calculators + educational content + subsidy guides + rent-vs-buy analysis + historical context. Existing tools (hyponamiru.cz, banky.cz, bank calculators) are transactional, not educational.
- **Timing is good.** Mortgage volumes up 41% in 2025, new regulations coming April 2026 (70% LTV for investors), new Housing Support Act (Jan 2026) - people need guidance navigating changes.
- **Proven model internationally.** CFPB's tools, Khan Academy's finance section, NerdWallet's early days all show demand for accessible mortgage education.
- **Research confirms impact.** Meta-analysis across 76 experiments: financial education improves both knowledge AND behavior. Financially literate borrowers pay lower rates and refinance at better times.

### The Caveats:
1. **AI-only content is SEO-risky.** SE Ranking experiment: 20 pure-AI sites initially ranked well, then lost ALL rankings in Feb 2025. Google rewards human expertise, especially in YMYL (Your Money Your Life) topics like mortgages. You MUST add genuine human expertise, not just review AI output.
2. **Financial content carries liability.** Even non-profit, you should add disclaimers and avoid personalized financial advice (that requires a license in CZ).
3. **Behavior change is hard.** People overestimate their financial knowledge. The site works best for people *already seeking help* - don't expect to reach the truly uninformed through organic search alone.

## 3. Is There a Better Way to Achieve the Same?

### Alternatives considered:

**A) YouTube channel / social media first**
- Pro: Lower barrier, faster feedback loop, reaches the "don't know they need help" audience
- Con: Doesn't provide interactive tools (the biggest gap), harder to maintain
- Verdict: **Complementary, not replacement.** Could drive traffic to the site.

**B) Partner with an existing platform (Bezrealitky, Sreality, Finmag)**
- Pro: Instant audience, credibility
- Con: Editorial control, potential conflicts of interest with advertisers
- Verdict: **Worth exploring later,** but start independent to build credibility.

**C) Mobile app instead of web**
- Pro: Better UX for calculators
- Con: Higher dev cost, lower discoverability, mortgage decisions aren't daily-use
- Verdict: **Web-first, PWA later if needed.** Mortgage research is desktop-heavy.

**D) Write a book / e-book**
- Pro: Authority building
- Con: Static, can't do interactive calculations, limited reach
- Verdict: **Could be a derivative product** from the site content.

**Recommended: Web-first, with social media as distribution channel.**

## 4. Distribution: How to Get Noticed Without Ads

Your worry is valid - content without distribution is useless. Here's the realistic playbook based on what worked for the projects above:

### Phase 1: Launch (weeks 1-4) - Seed traffic
- **Czech Reddit (r/czech, r/praha)** - Post your Rent vs Buy calculator results as interesting data, not as self-promotion. "I calculated whether it's cheaper to rent or buy in Prague in 2026, here are the numbers..."
- **Facebook groups** - Czech personal finance groups (Investiční club, Finanční gramotnost), housing groups. Same approach: share insights, not links.
- **Twitter/X** - Czech fintwit community. Share surprising calculations, mortgage myths debunked.
- **Friends & family** - Genuinely useful calculators get shared. If someone is house-hunting, they'll send the Rent vs Buy calculator to friends.

### Phase 2: SEO (months 2-6) - Organic growth engine
- Czech-language YMYL financial content has FAR less competition than English
- Target long-tail: "kolik stojí hypotéka celkem", "vyplatí se nájem nebo hypotéka", "státní podpora bydlení 2026"
- Each calculator page is an SEO landing page with unique, non-replicable content
- **Finanztip gets 70% of traffic from organic search** - this is the proven path

### Phase 3: Authority (months 4-12)
- **Media outreach** - Czech journalists covering housing (Hospodářské noviny, Ekonom, Aktuálně.cz) need data for articles. Offer your calculators/data as source.
- **Embeddable calculator widgets** - Let real estate sites, blogs, forums embed your calculators (with backlink). CFPB's tools are embedded everywhere.
- **Newsletter** - Finanztip has 750K subscribers, MoneySavingExpert has 14M. Email is the highest-ROI channel ($42 return per $1 invested).

### Realistic expectation
- Month 1: 100-500 visitors (friends, Reddit posts, early SEO indexing)
- Month 3: 500-2,000 visitors (SEO kicking in, some social shares)
- Month 6: 2,000-10,000 visitors (if content is genuinely useful and SEO works)
- The hockey stick comes from SEO compound effect - Finanztip took years to reach 3M/month

## 5. Is Building Content Really the Easy Part?

**Honest answer: the content is the easier part, but not trivial.**

What's genuinely easy with LLM:
- Drafting educational articles explaining mortgage concepts
- Generating comparison tables
- Writing subsidy guides from government sources
- Creating FAQ content

What's harder than it looks:
- **Calculator accuracy** - A rent-vs-buy calculator needs to model: mortgage payments, property appreciation, maintenance costs, opportunity cost of down payment, tax implications, insurance, transaction costs, inflation. Getting this right matters - wrong numbers destroy trust.
- **Keeping data current** - Rates change, subsidies change, regulations change. Need a system for flagging stale content.
- **Making it engaging** - Only 5% of people remember statistics, but 63% remember stories. Dry calculator + article format isn't enough. Need relatable scenarios ("Pavel, 29, earns 45K/month, wants a 2+kk in Brno...").
- **SEO for YMYL** - Google holds financial content to higher standard. Need real author identity, citations, E-E-A-T signals.

**Bottom line:** Content creation is maybe 30% of the work. Distribution + trust-building + keeping it accurate is 70%.

## 6. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Google penalizes AI content (YMYL) | Add real author bio, cite Czech data sources (CNB, CZSO), get expert reviews, add unique datasets/calculators that AI can't fake |
| Low organic traffic initially | Launch with 3-5 genuinely useful calculators (shareable), write for Czech finance communities (Peníze.cz forum, Reddit r/czech, Facebook groups) |
| Nobody cares | Validate BEFORE building everything: post a manual rent-vs-buy calculation on Reddit/Facebook, see if people engage. If 0 interest, pivot. |
| Liability for financial advice | Clear disclaimers, no personalized recommendations, link to licensed advisors |
| Content becomes outdated | Auto-update rate data from CNB/Hypoindex APIs, flag articles for review when market conditions change |
| Sustainability (it's non-profit) | Keep costs near-zero: static site on Vercel free tier, LLM costs are one-time, your time is the main cost |
